Kabuki Theatre

Origins

Performed first by Izumo no Okuni in 17th century Kyoto

Originally done only by women with heavy emphasis on ribald themes and ties to prostitution

All-male performances established in the late 17th century

Formalized during Golden Age of 1700s as an official form of theatre

Performers

Every actor adopts a distinct stage name, passed down through generations

A 'Shumei' is held as a ceremony to grant stage names upon new actors and those changing major role types

Performance

A 'Hanamichi' (flower path) extends the stage into the crowd

Stage tricks are used for dramatic effects, referred to as 'Keren'

Subjects

'Jidai-mono': historical/samurai stories

'Sewa-mono': domestic/townspeople stories

'Shosagoto': dance pieces

Famous Works

Kanadehon Chushingura (Treasury of Loyal Retainers)

Yoshitsune Senbon Zakura (Yoshitsune and the Thousand Cherry Trees)

Sugawara Denju Tenarai Kagami (Sugawara and the Secrets of Calligraphy)
